🌱 Sowing the Seeds of Love — Bedtime App
Request #001 · Submitted March 24, 2026
💬 Aiden Responded
Your Vision
A calm, family-centered bedtime app for children ages 3–10. Nightly rhythm: Scripture → repetition → optional prayer → parent blessing. Peace over performance. Privacy-first, child-safe. Vision: eventually offer this to all parents.
✅ Your Answers
Who is this for?

Bible translation preference
Parent-selectable — use Bible Gateway as reference. Research free APIs for commercial use.
Verse selection
Theme-based reference suggestions + ability for parents to look up and choose verses themselves.
Beta testers
Extended family — testing internally first.
💬 Aiden's Response — March 25, 2026
GiGi — we've done our homework, and this is more buildable than you might think.

Here's what we found on the Bible API front. There's a service called API.Bible (run by the American Bible Society) that gives free developer access to over 1,500 Bible versions across 1,000+ languages — including KJV, WEB (World English Bible), and many others. For an app meant to serve all parents, this is the right foundation. Some popular modern translations (like NIV) require commercial licensing, but there are excellent, widely-loved options available at no cost. We'll build the parent selection experience around those.

We also found bible-api.com — a simple open-source API that's fully free and great for early prototyping. We'll use that to get the first version running fast, then graduate to API.Bible as the product scales.

Before we start building, we'd love to understand a few more things about your vision:

A few questions from Aiden:

1. The bigger vision — free or paid?
When you imagine this reaching all parents someday, do you see it as a free gift to families, a paid app (like $1.99 in the App Store), or something supported by donations or a church/ministry? There's no wrong answer — it just shapes how we build from the start.

2. Where do families use it?
Are you thinking of this primarily on a phone, a tablet by the bedside, or both? Should it work offline (no internet needed at bedtime)? This affects whether we build a website app or a native iOS/Android app.

3. What themes matter most for Version 1?
If a parent opens the app for the first time and browses "themes," what would you want them to find? Things like: God's Love, Courage, Peace, Forgiveness, Gratitude, Strength? What feels most essential for young children?

4. How personal should it feel?
Should the app know the child's name and use it ("Tonight's verse is for you, Emma...")? Or do you prefer it feel universal and not require any personal information — keeping it simple and private?

Take your time with these. And if it's easier to just talk it through, hit the chat button below — that works too.

— Aiden
